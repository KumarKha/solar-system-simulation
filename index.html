Terrestrial

Mercury

Planet Type:

Terrestrial

Distance from Sun:

~58 million km (36 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

167°C (333°F)

Orbital Speed:

~47.9 km/s

Orbit Period:

~0.24 Earth years (88 days)

Description:

Mercury is the smallest planet and orbits closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere to trap heat, so temperatures swing wildly between extreme heat and freezing cold.The surface is rocky and heavily cratered, much like the Moon.

Fun Fact:

A day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts 176 Earth days!

Venus

Planet Type:

Terrestrial

Distance from Sun:

~108 million km (67 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

464°C (867°F)

Orbital Speed:

~35 km/s

Orbit Period:

~0.62 Earth years (225 days)

Description:

Venus is Earth's twin in size but has a dense, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet. Its surface is volcanic and shrouded in clouds of acid.

Fun Fact:

A day on Venus is longer than a year, and it spins backward!

Earth

Planet Type:

Terrestrial

Distance from Sun:

~150 million km (93 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

15°C (59°F)

Orbital Speed:

~29.8 km/s

Orbit Period:

1 Earth year (365.25 days)

Description:

The only planet known to support life, Earth has a breathable atmosphere, liquid water, and a protective magnetic field. It's just the right distance from the Sun — the "Goldilocks zone."

Fun Fact:

Earth is the only planet not named after a god.

Mars

Planet Type:

Terrestrial

Distance from Sun:

~228 million km (142 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

-65°C (-85°F)

Orbital Speed:

~24.1 km/s

Orbit Period:

~1.88 Earth years

Description:

Known as the Red Planet, Mars has dusty deserts, polar ice caps, and signs of ancient rivers. It's the most Earth-like planet and a top candidate for future human exploration.

Fun Fact:

Mars has the tallest volcano in the solar system — Olympus Mons!

Giants

Jupiter

Planet Type:

Gas Giant

Distance from Sun:

~778 million km (484 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

-110°C (-166°F)

Orbital Speed:

~13.1 km/s

Orbit Period:

~11.86 Earth years

Description:

Jupiter is the largest planet, with a swirling atmosphere and a massive storm called the Great Red Spot. It has more than 90 moons and a strong magnetic field.

Fun Fact:

Jupiter's moon Ganymede is larger than Mercury!

Saturn

Planet Type:

Gas Giant

Distance from Sun:

~1.43 billion km (889 million mi)

Avg Temperature:

-140°C (-220°F)

Orbital Speed:

~9.7 km/s

Orbit Period:

~29.45 Earth years

Description:

Famous for its dazzling ring system, Saturn is a giant made of mostly hydrogen and helium. It has over 140 moons, including the fascinating Titan.

Fun Fact:

Saturn is so light it could float in water!

Uranus

Planet Type:

Ice Giant

Distance from Sun:

~2.87 billion km (1.78 billion mi)

Avg Temperature:

-195°C (-319°F)

Orbital Speed:

~6.8 km/s

Orbit Period:

~84 Earth years

Description:

Uranus is an ice giant with a pale blue tint and a bizarre tilt — it spins on its side! This extreme axis gives it the most unusual seasons in the solar system.

Fun Fact:

A season on Uranus lasts 21 Earth years.

Neptune

Planet Type:

Ice Giant

Distance from Sun:

~4.5 billion km (2.8 billion mi)

Avg Temperature:

-200°C (-328°F)

Orbital Speed:

~5.4 km/s

Orbit Period:

~164.8 Earth years

Description:

The farthest known planet, Neptune is deep blue and full of storms. Despite its distance, it has the fastest winds recorded in the solar system.

Fun Fact:

Winds on Neptune can reach 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph)!
